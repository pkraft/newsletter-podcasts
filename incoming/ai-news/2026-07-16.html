Models
Thinking Machines releases Inkling, its first open model

Thinking Machines Lab released Inkling, a 975-billion-parameter mixture-of-experts model with 41 billion active parameters, multimodal reasoning, and a one-million-token context window. It ships open-weight — developers can download and customize the weights — and is available for fine-tuning through the company's Tinker platform alongside a smaller preview version. This is the lab's first foundation model, arriving more than a year after Mira Murati founded the company on a record $2 billion seed round at a $12 billion valuation.

Why it matters: The most-watched startup in AI finally showed its hand, and instead of chasing frontier benchmarks it's positioning as the customizable, cost-balanced open alternative — a direct play for the enterprise fine-tuning market.

Moonshot unveils Kimi K3, the largest open model ever released

China's Moonshot AI announced Kimi K3, a 2.8-trillion-parameter open mixture-of-experts model — the first open model in the three-trillion class. Built on Kimi Delta Attention and Attention Residuals, it activates 16 of 896 experts per token, is multimodal, and carries a one-million-token context window. It's live now in Moonshot's Kimi apps and API, with full weights promised on July 27.

Why it matters: Chinese labs keep raising the open-weights ceiling faster than US labs are willing to match, and a 2.8-trillion-parameter release turns "open frontier-scale models" from a talking point into a downloadable fact.

Anthropic is planning investor meetings ahead of a potential IPO later this year, with Goldman Sachs, Morgan Stanley, and JPMorgan Chase leading the effort. Built on the acquisition of AI engineering startup Fractional AI, Ode starts with 100 engineers, works closely with Anthropic on custom systems, and stays open to using competing models. Why it matters: The frontier labs have concluded that the bottleneck to the next trillion dollars is implementation, not models — and they're now vertically integrating into consulting to capture it.

Apple Intelligence approved for China — powered by Alibaba's Qwen

China's Cyberspace Administration approved Apple Intelligence for launch, with Apple relying on Alibaba's Qwen model rather than its own AI to power features across iOS, iPadOS, macOS, and visionOS. The deal follows stalled talks with Baidu and evaluations of DeepSeek and ByteDance models, and lands as Apple's Greater China revenue jumped 28 percent to $20.5 billion last quarter.

Innovation
First experimental evidence of recursive self-improvement in an AI agent

Researchers ran an automated research agent on its own harness for eight days: an inner loop optimized code against an eval while an outer loop rewrote the inner agent's harness to maximize its average benchmark score. The fully autonomous system beat the harness the team had hand-tuned for two years, designed a novel search algorithm, cut prompt size sixteen-fold, and layered its own defenses against reward hacking.

Why it matters: Recursive self-improvement has been the defining thought experiment of AI risk for two decades; this is a small, bounded, but real empirical instance of it working better than human engineering.

OpenAI's GPT-Red attacks its own models to make them safer

OpenAI trained GPT-Red, a model that iteratively generates adversarial prompts to expose vulnerabilities in other models at scale. Folding GPT-Red's attacks into training reportedly cut failures on a difficult prompt-injection benchmark sixfold for GPT-5.6 Sol.

Why it matters: Automated red-teaming turns safety testing from a manual bottleneck into something that scales with compute — and a sixfold improvement on prompt injection addresses one of the biggest blockers to deploying agents with real permissions.

Perplexity's SPACE: secure sandboxes for AI agents

Perplexity detailed SPACE, a sandbox platform for AI agents handling sensitive tasks. It runs agents in ephemeral sandboxes destroyed after each task, isolates credentials behind a control plane and node-level services, and adds rolling snapshots and encrypted storage, with support for on-premises and offline operation.

Why it matters: Agent security infrastructure is becoming its own product category — the industry is building the containment layer that lets agents touch real credentials and real systems without betting the company on model behavior.
