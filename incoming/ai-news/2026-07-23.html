Tools

OpenAI launches Presence, a platform for deploying enterprise AI agents

OpenAI Presence lets enterprises launch and manage real-time voice agents and chatbots across customer support and internal operations, pairing model reasoning with permissions, policies, evaluations, escalation rules, and post-deployment improvement tools. It gives organizations centralized deployment, orchestration, monitoring, and governance for agents running in production.

Why it matters: OpenAI is packaging agent governance — not just agent capability — as a product, an acknowledgment that enterprise adoption is bottlenecked on control and oversight, not raw model quality.

Cursor ships Router, an intelligent model router that cuts costs 60%

Cursor Router automatically classifies each coding request by task type and complexity, then routes it to the most cost-effective model capable of handling it — frontier models for demanding work, cheaper models for the rest. Trained on more than 600,000 live requests, early-access enterprise accounts saw 30–50% cost savings on routed requests versus sending everything to Opus 4.8, with no drop in output quality. It's rolling out now to Teams and Enterprise plans across desktop, web, iOS, CLI, and the SDK.

Why it matters: Model routing at this scale is a bet that "which model" is becoming an infrastructure decision to automate away, not a choice developers should have to make request by request.

Models

Google DeepMind ships Gemini 3.5 Flash Cyber, a specialized vulnerability-hunting model

DeepMind released Gemini 3.5 Flash Cyber, a lightweight cybersecurity-focused model built on Gemini 3.5 Flash and fine-tuned to find, validate, and patch software vulnerabilities. It's rolling out through a limited-access CodeMender pilot for governments and trusted partners, with CodeMender capabilities also becoming available through the Gemini Enterprise Agent Platform.

Why it matters: A purpose-built model for automated vulnerability discovery and patching arrives the same week frontier models were shown breaking out of their own sandboxes to attack real infrastructure — DeepMind is racing to make defense keep pace with offense.

US Department of Energy and Arcee AI launch Genesis-Science-1, an open-weight science model

The Department of Energy, working with startup Arcee AI, announced Genesis-Science-1 (GS1), an open-weight model aimed at scientific computing workflows with an emphasis on reproducible results. Arcee will lead model development while DOE scientists supply domain expertise, data, and research tasks; the project is soliciting outside contributions for pretraining, post-training, and evaluation, with its first contribution window closing August 6.

Why it matters: A federal science agency co-developing an open-weight foundation model — rather than just funding academic research — is a notable template for how government could shape AI development going forward.

Staffing

Amazon cuts jobs in its Artificial General Intelligence unit

Amazon eliminated roles within its Artificial General Intelligence group as part of broader restructuring, even as the company says it remains committed to investing in AI. The cuts come amid a wider wave of AI-driven reorganizations across tech and follow a string of leadership changes inside Amazon's AI efforts.

Why it matters: Layoffs inside the team explicitly named after AGI — while the company reaffirms AI investment — is a reminder that "cutting for AI" and "cutting the AI team" are now happening at the same companies simultaneously.

Business

OpenAI raises AI infrastructure spending target to $750 billion

OpenAI now plans to spend $750 billion on computing infrastructure through 2030, up from an earlier $600 billion estimate, as it takes greater direct control over data-center development. The escalation comes as its original Stargate joint-venture plans face delays, pushing OpenAI toward projects like a $20 billion, 3.2-gigawatt campus in Georgia that it will fund and operate more directly. The figure was reported consistently across multiple outlets today.

Why it matters: A quarter-trillion-dollar upward revision in five years signals OpenAI is betting its business on being infrastructure-constrained rather than demand-constrained — a wager that raises both the ceiling on what's possible and the stakes if revenue growth doesn't keep pace.

AMD scores a breakout week — Microsoft picks Helios for Azure, Anthropic signs multibillion-dollar chip deal

Days after unveiling its Helios rack-scale AI system, AMD landed two major wins in one week. Microsoft Azure will deploy Helios racks — built on MI455X GPUs and EPYC "Venice" CPUs — for AI inference workloads, joining Meta, OpenAI, and Oracle as early adopters and marking a real diversification away from Microsoft's near-total reliance on Nvidia. Separately, Anthropic signed a deal worth tens of billions of dollars to buy up to 2 gigawatts of AMD's Instinct MI450 chips starting in the first half of next year, with AMD investing up to $5 billion into Anthropic as deployment milestones are hit.

Why it matters: Two of the industry's most consequential AI buyers picking AMD in the same week is the clearest signal yet that Nvidia's compute dominance — still north of 95% of the data-center GPU market — is starting to crack under sheer demand and price pressure.

Microsoft and Mistral expand strategic partnership for regulated-industry AI

Microsoft and Mistral deepened their partnership to bring Mistral's frontier and efficient models into Microsoft's enterprise products, including a multibillion-dollar expansion of Europe-based GPU infrastructure. Mistral Medium 3.5 and OCR 4 are landing in Microsoft Foundry, with Mistral Medium 3.5 also coming to Copilot Studio, and deployment options spanning Azure, Azure Local, and fully disconnected environments.

Why it matters: The deal doubles down on giving regulated industries and European customers a frontier AI option that isn't OpenAI or Google — sovereignty and compliance are becoming as important a sales pitch as raw capability.
